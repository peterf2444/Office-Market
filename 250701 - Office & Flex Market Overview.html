The Closing Window: Leasing Dynamics Are Shifting

Key indicators show that the market is beginning to favor landlords. The period of peak tenant advantage and record-high concessions is showing signs of ending.

Concession Packages Tapering Off

After five consecutive years of growth, landlord concession packages (free rent and tenant improvement allowances) have begun to level off and even decline slightly in Q1 2025, signaling a turn in the market.

Trophy Rents Are Climbing

Both base rents and net effective rents for Trophy assets saw a sharp increase in Q1 2025, a leading indicator for where the rest of the high-quality market is headed.

Lease Terms Are Extending

The average lease term for Trophy properties has been trending upwards, reaching 118 months. Longer terms signal growing landlord confidence and less flexibility for tenants.

Source: Avison Young, Q1 2025 U.S. Office Report

Market Spotlight: San Francisco's Rebound

San Francisco serves as a prime case study for the market's recovery. After a period of correction, its office market and broader economy are showing strong signs of a robust, tech-fueled resurgence.

Office Market Turning the Corner

  • ✓
    Record Leasing: Q1 2025 saw 3.4M sq ft leased, the highest quarterly total in 10 years.
  • ✓
    Flight to Quality: Class A properties accounted for 69% of total leasing activity in the first quarter, showing strong demand for premier space.
  • ✓
    Sublease Inventory Shrinks: Sublease availability dropped 13.6% year-over-year, signaling tightening supply.
  • ✓
    Large Deals Return: Eleven leases over 100,000 sq ft were signed in 2024, nearly triple the number from 2023.

Broad Economic Vitality

  • ✓
    Tech Dominance: Tech companies accounted for 50% of all leasing activity in 2024.
  • ✓
    VC Investment Hub: The Bay Area attracted nearly half of all U.S. venture capital in 2024.
  • ✓
    Job Growth Rebounds: The city is adding thousands of jobs, with unemployment at a low 3.7%.
  • ✓
    Tourism & Conventions Roar Back: Visitor spending is projected to reach $9.4B in 2025, driving economic activity.

Strategic Outlook: A Market at an Inflection Point

The data reveals a clear narrative: the U.S. office market has reached a turning point. The era of tenant advantage is giving way to a more balanced, and increasingly competitive, landscape.

The Market Has Tightened

Key indicators like rising rents for Trophy assets, declining landlord concessions, and positive absorption in prime markets show that tenant-favorable conditions are waning.

Quality Space is Already Scarce

The "hidden tightness" in desirable markets like San Francisco, Irvine, and Bellevue reveals that the inventory companies actually want is limited and in high demand.

The Strategic Role of Flex Space

The coworking sector is not just growing, it's actively absorbing market share, with operators expanding aggressively to meet surging corporate demand in key tech hubs.

The convergence of these trends suggests the window for securing favorable deals on prime assets is currently open, but as the market tightens, opportunities may become more competitive.
